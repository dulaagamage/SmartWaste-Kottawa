Key Waste Issues in Kottawa

Illegal Dumping

Illegal waste disposal continues to be a pressing concern in Kottawa, where garbage is often dumped in unregulated open spaces, abandoned lots, or roadsides. This not only diminishes the area's visual appeal, but also poses serious health risks to residents due to pest infestations, contamination, and foul odors. Strict monitoring and community reporting tools are essential to tackle this ongoing issue.

Low Recycling Rates

A significant portion of household and commercial waste in Kottawa ends up in landfills instead of being recycled. Despite growing environmental awareness, infrastructure such as color-coded bins, sorting stations, and municipal collection programs remain underdeveloped. This hinders proper recycling and contributes to unnecessary environmental burden.

Lack of Awareness about Sustainable Disposal Practices

Many residents are unaware of how to responsibly sort, store, and dispose of their waste. Improper disposal of electronics, plastics, and organic matter leads to both pollution and missed opportunities for reuse. Without targeted education campaigns, community workshops, and school-level programs, sustainable waste habits will remain limited.
